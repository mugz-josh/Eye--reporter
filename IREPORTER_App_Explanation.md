# IREPORTER App: A Simple Guide for Everyone

## What is IREPORTER?

Imagine you're walking down the street and you see a big pothole that could damage cars, or you notice some government workers acting suspiciously. What do you do? With IREPORTER, you can easily report these issues right from your phone or computer!

IREPORTER is like a digital "report card" for your community. It lets regular people like you tell the government about problems that need fixing, or warn them about corruption. It's designed to make it easy for citizens to speak up and hold authorities accountable.

## The Two Main Types of Reports

### 1. Red-Flag Reports üö©
These are for serious problems like:
- Corruption (like a government official taking bribes)
- Crime or illegal activities
- Safety hazards that could hurt people

**Example:** You see a police officer asking for money to "speed up" a traffic ticket. That's corruption! You can report it as a red flag.

### 2. Intervention Reports üîß
These are for things that need government help to fix:
- Broken roads or bridges
- Flooding problems
- Schools or hospitals that need repairs
- Any public service that's not working properly

**Example:** The street light near your house hasn't worked for months, making the area dangerous at night. That's something the government should fix!

## How the App Works: A Simple Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Getting Started
1. **Sign Up** - Create your account with your name and email
2. **Log In** - Use your email and password to get into the app
3. **You're Ready!** - Now you can start reporting issues

### Step 2: Creating a Report
1. **Choose Your Report Type** - Red flag or intervention?
2. **Tell Your Story** - Write what happened and why it's important
3. **Add Location** - The app can find your location automatically, or you can choose it on a map
4. **Add Photos/Videos** - Take pictures or videos to show the problem (optional)
5. **Submit** - Send your report to the system

### Step 3: What Happens Next?
Your report goes into the system with a status of "Draft" - this means it's saved but not yet official.

## The Report Status Journey

Every report goes through different stages, like a journey:

### üìù Draft
- Your report is saved but you can still change it
- You can edit the details, add more photos, or change the location
- You can even delete it if you change your mind

### üîç Under Investigation
- An admin (government official) has seen your report
- They're looking into the problem
- You can't change the report anymore
- This is when real action starts happening!

### ‚ùå Rejected
- The admin looked at your report but decided it's not valid
- Maybe it was a misunderstanding, or not enough information
- You get a notification explaining why

### ‚úÖ Resolved
- The problem has been fixed!
- The government took action and solved the issue
- You get a notification that it's done

## Who Uses the App?

### Regular Users (Like You!)

### Admins (Government Officials)
